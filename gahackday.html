background

Voice User Interfaces (VUIs) are becoming increasingly common on our phones, computers and other devices. Amazon's Alexa voice interface has become a "sleeper hit" (The Guardian) by focusing on use cases within the home. 

problem

Speaking to a device can still be awkward, though, especially when there are other people around. Design and deploy an Alexa skill that addresses this social disconnect. How can VUI integrate into a family or other group? In a social context, what activities are well-suited to VUI, as opposed to another type of human-computer interface? 

solution

CalPal: A tool that helps you convert calories to time exercise.

By utilizing this tool, you can better understand how much exercise is required to burn off the calories you may be thinking of eating. By lowering the bar and making it easier to comprehend workout time, healthier eating decisions are only a question away.

discovery || research phase

Focus on Minimum Viable Product as a team

    Considertion was given to the following:

    • Fully fucntion within 8 hours
    • No data mining/scraping. No database building
    • Includes error cases
    • Meets judging criteria + fulfilles deliverables

ideation
  • We looked at ways to engage groups of people without incorporating gamification. Removing complicated rules or long strings of speach from Alexa was given priority.
  • Pros/Cons, A|B Testing and User Surveys were incorperated into initial ideation. Focus given to creating a skill that has broad range of appeal - "cool" skills were tossed out over "practical" skills.

“If plan ‘A’ fails – remember you have 25 letters left.” – Chris Guillebeau

agreed to use User Centered Design
    • The best design is based upon an explicit understanding of users, tasks and environments
    • Users are involved throughout design and development
    • The design is driven and refined by user-centered evaluation
    • The process is iterative. Reinterative, reinterate, reinterate was the mantra
    • The design addresses the whole user experience

Agile Principle of timeboxing was established from the start. 2 hour sprints for deployable skills were the goal, with UX Designers reporting back results of Usability Testing every 1 hour

Contextual story
  • A contextual story was created directly from User Surveys 
  • As a UX Designer, I work mostly with target groups (or personas) and why these target groups need a feature, than with the feature itself. Ensuring that we only build the features that makes sense building from a target groups perspective, thus helping us building something that people actually want.
  • Stuctured Contextual Story based on:   As a (persona) || I want (feature) || So that (benefit for the user - w/ measurable) || In order to (impact/benefit for company - w/ measurable

Design || Testing prototypes Phase 

introducing calpal - original interation

      Invocation Name: [Start/Launch/Open/Ask] CalPal

      Sample Interaction -  Alexa, ask CalPal

      • How many [w-unit] would it take to burn [n(cal)] by [activity]?
      • How long would it take me to burn [n(cal)] by [activity]?
      • How long would I have to [activity] to burn [n(cal)]?
      • If I eat [n(cal)], how long would I have to [activity] to burn it off?

      Sample Alexa Response

    • To burn [n(cal)], you would need to [activity] for about [time]
