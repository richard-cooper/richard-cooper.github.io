alarm clock concept

Alarm clock concept - 1 week sprint - focus on the skill of user interviews

overview

Project type: individual

  • MY ROLE: research, ideation, Testing, prototyping

skills used: user interviews, A|B testing, usability testing, interaction design, sketching, lowfi mockup

  • tools used: pen + paper, invision

background

With a 1 week sprint to learn how to conduct a valuable User Interview in which a mobile app could be designed based entirely on qualitative data gathered in a 20 minute interview of a stranger.

problem

Provided with a limited amount of time, with a limited amount interaction, zero follow up and no prior knowledge - can a carefully crafted Interview Script be crafted to ensure all qualitative data is gathered to create an app designed to resolve a point point of a stranger.

solution

GoToSleep allows an alarm to be set in which the phone will automatically shut itself off at a predetermined time, forcing the user to put the phone down and focus on sleeping, instead of browsing the internet.

discovery || research phase

Original interview guidelines - focusing on skill building

    Consideration was given to the following:

    • Document the questions that need answers
    • Plan for how to elicit responses that are qualitative
    • Plan on follow up questions that keep the subject speaking
    • Plan on how to provide empathy

Affinity Diagram
  • After conducting a thorough user interview, I  created an affinity diagram by grouping "I" statements
  • Developed a proto-persona that had feelings, goals, tasks and pain points

PErsona - Phil
    • Phil is an international traveler who has family and friends across the globe. He often stays up late browsing the internet in an effort to be able to be in a timezone in which his family and friends are also in. This leaves him tired and unable perform his best.

"My friends are important to me,  yeah, I'll sacrifice a little to keep in touch - thats what friends do."

Outcome and Next steps

  • Final product focused on creating an MVP in which an alarm could be set to automatically turn the phone off at a predetermined time.
  • Learned to dig deeper in user interviews for more in-depth answers. Qualitative data.
  • Learned it more productive to reiterate often, designing for a single feature at a time.
